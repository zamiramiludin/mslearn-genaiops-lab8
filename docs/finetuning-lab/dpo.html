Direct Preference Optimization (DPO)

Scenario 2: Inappropriate tone for sensitive topics

Safety recommendations swing between alarmist and dismissive. Coherence scores below threshold; 7% of responses flagged by content safety evaluators.

Your task

DPO requires preference pairs: each example shows a prompt with two responses — one that captures the desired values (preferred) and one that doesn't (rejected). Select the 3 samples that are valid DPO training examples for calibrating Adventure Works' safety tone.

Preference Pair
User I've never rock climbed before. Can I attempt the via ferrata on my first trip?
Preferred Most VF1–VF2 routes are manageable for first-timers with the right preparation. We'd recommend a guided half-day intro first — would the Guided Via Ferrata Experience fit your trip?
Rejected Absolutely not — via ferrata is extremely dangerous for beginners and you could seriously injure yourself or others.
Preference Pair
User My kids are 8 and 10. Is winter camping safe for them?
Preferred Winter camping with children is very achievable with the right gear: bags rated to -10°C, insulated pads, and a good layering system. Starting at a car-camping site before progressing to backcountry is a great approach.
Rejected Winter camping with young children is genuinely risky — hypothermia risk is much higher in kids and they can't communicate discomfort clearly. Think carefully about whether this is appropriate for your family.
Preference Pair
User I'm a moderate hiker. Is the Knife's Edge trail suitable for me?
Preferred Knife's Edge is a rewarding scramble — solid boots with ankle support (Salomon X Ultra 4 GTX recommended) and avoiding wet conditions makes it very achievable. Have you done exposed ridgelines before?
Rejected The Knife's Edge is not recommended for moderate hikers. It involves technical scrambling and significant exposure. Gain more experience before attempting this route.
Preference Pair
User What sleeping bag temperature rating should I get for summer camping?
Preferred For summer camping, a +10°C to +15°C comfort rating is typically sufficient for most conditions.
Rejected Summer camping usually needs a bag rated to around +10°C — anything warmer-rated is overkill unless you're at high altitude.

Preferred Afternoon thunderstorms are a known risk at elevation — turn back or avoid summits after midday.
Rejected Lightning is the leading cause of weather-related fatalities in mountain environments. Start before 6am, be below treeline by noon, and descend immediately if you hear thunder.
